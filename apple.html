Fruit Info

Apple:

About an Apple:

  • An apple is a healthy fruit that grows on trees.
  • Apples can be red, green, or yellow.
  • They are sweet and crunchy when you bite them
  • If you cut an apple in half, you will find tiny brown seeds in the middle.
  • Eating the peel is essential, as it contains half of the fiber and the majority of the antioxidants.

Benefits:

  • Heart Health: Apples contain fiber and special antioxidants that help lower "bad" cholesterol. This keeps your heart pumping easily and your blood flowing smoothly.
  • Immune System Support: They are a great source of Vitamin C. This acts like a shield for your body, helping you fight off colds and heal your skin after a scrape.
  • Better Digestion: The skin of an apple is full of pectin (a type of fiber). Think of it like a gentle broom that cleans out your stomach and keeps your digestion regular.
  • Natural Energy Boost: Instead of a sugary snack that makes you tired later, apples have natural sugars and fiber. This gives you steady energy for your brain and muscles all afternoon.
  • Weight & Fullness: Because apples are mostly water and fiber, they help you feel full and satisfied. This makes them the perfect snack to keep your body at a healthy weight
  • Healthy Smile: Chewing a crisp apple stimulates saliva in your mouth, which washes away bacteria and helps keep your teeth and gums clean.

Nutrition Factor:

  • Calories: Contains approximately 95 calories, providing a healthy, low-energy snack that keeps you fueled without being heavy.
  • High Fiber: Provides about 4.5 grams of fiber. This includes pectin, a soluble fiber that aids digestion and helps maintain healthy cholesterol levels.
  • Vitamin C: Supplies roughly 14% of the Daily Value of Vitamin C. This antioxidant supports the immune system and helps the body repair tissues.
  • Rich in Potassium: Contains about 195 mg of potassium, an essential mineral that supports heart health and nerve function.
  • Natural Sugars: Provides about 19 grams of natural sugar (fructose). Unlike processed sugar, this is released slowly into the bloodstream due to the fiber content, preventing energy crashes.
  • Antioxidants: Packed with plant compounds like quercetin and catechin. These are mostly found in the skin and help protect cells from damage.
  • High Water Content: Composed of about 86% water, which helps with daily hydration and keeps you feeling full longer.
  • Fat & Sodium Free: Apples contain zero fat, cholesterol, or sodium, making them an ideal heart-healthy choice for any diet.

Did you know?

  • Family Ties: Apples are actually part of the rose family (Rosaceae).
  • Floating Fruit: About 25% of an apple's volume is air, which is why they float—perfect for the game of "bobbing for apples".
  • The 7,500 Rule: There are over 7,500 varieties of apples grown worldwide. If you ate a new variety every day, it would take you over 20 years to try them all!
  • Ancient Travelers: Apples originated in Central Asia and were brought to other parts of the world by traders on the Silk Road.
