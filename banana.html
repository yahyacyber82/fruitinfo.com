Fruit Info

Banana:

About a Banana:

  • A banana is a healthy fruit that grows in large hanging bunches on tropical plants.
  • Most bananas turn bright yellow when they are ready to eat, but some can be green or even red.
  • They have a creamy texture and a very sweet flavor that kids and adults love.
  • When you peel a banana, the fruit is soft and creamy with tiny, harmless black specks in the center.
  • Known as "nature's power bar," bananas give you a quick boost to help you out with your day

Benefits:

  • Brain Power: The Vitamin B6 in bananas helps kids (and adults!) concentrate better during lessons or work.
  • Happy Stomach: Bananas are very gentle on the stomach and help your body digest food easily.
  • Mood Booster: They contain a special nutrient that the body turns into serotonin, which is a chemical that helps you feel happy and relaxed.
  • Muscle Recovery: Athletes eat bananas because the potassium helps prevent muscle cramps after running or playing sports.

Nutrition Factor:

  • Calories: Contains about 105 calories, making it a perfect "on-the-go" energy snack.
  • High Potassium: Provides roughly 422 mg of potassium. This mineral is vital for heart health and helps your muscles move properly.
  • itamin B6: Supplies about 33% of the Daily Value. This helps your brain stay sharp and your body create new red blood cells.
  • Vitamin C: Provides about 10-12% of your daily needs, which helps protect your cells and supports your immune system.
  • Fiber (Pectin): Contains about 3 grams of fiber. This helps with digestion and keeps you feeling full.
  • Natural Sugars: Packed with healthy sugars (sucrose, fructose, and glucose) that provide steady energy.
  • Fat & Sodium Free: Like apples, bananas have zero fat and zero sodium, making them very heart-friendly.

Fun Facts:

  • Floating Fruit: Just like apples, bananas will float in water because they are less dense than the water itself.
  • Giant Herbs: Even though they can grow 20 feet tall, banana plants are actually giant herbs, not trees.
  • Technically a Berry: In botany, a banana is classified as a berry, while a strawberry is not!
  • Original Packaging: A banana comes in its own biodegradable wrapper (the peel), making it a perfect zero-waste snack.
